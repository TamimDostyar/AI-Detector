About Our AI Text Detector

Understanding how our model works and how to interpret results

Our Approach

Hybrid Detection Model

Our AI text detector uses a hybrid approach that combines two powerful machine learning models:

Statistical Model

Uses TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) features to analyze word patterns and frequencies that distinguish human vs. AI writing. This captures linguistic nuances and stylistic patterns.

Deep Learning Model

A neural network trained on sequence data to capture deeper contextual patterns in text. This model understands sentence structures and longer-range dependencies in the text.

Training Data

Our models were trained on a diverse dataset containing:

  • Human-written essays and articles from various sources and domains
  • AI-generated text from different large language models (LLMs)
  • Various writing styles, topics, and complexity levels

Both models were trained to distinguish patterns unique to AI generation versus human writing, considering factors like repetition, coherence, complexity, and stylistic markers.

How to Interpret Results

When you analyze text with our detector, we calculate two separate probabilities:

Calculation Method:

  1. The statistical model produces a probability (stat_p_human) that the text was written by a human
  2. The deep learning model produces its own probability (dl_p_human) that the text was human-written
  3. We average these two probabilities to get a final human probability score:
p_human = (stat_p_human + dl_p_human) / 2

Then we apply a decision rule:

  • If p_human > 0.5, we classify the text as "Human" with confidence p_human
  • Otherwise, we classify it as "AI" with confidence 1 - p_human

Reading the Results

When our detector shows "50% human", it literally means our model estimates a 50% probability that the text was written by a human. The remaining 50% represents the probability it was AI-generated.

Example: If our detector shows "75% human", it means:

  • 75% confidence the text was written by a human
  • 25% confidence the text was AI-generated

Limitations

While our detector is powerful, it's important to understand its limitations:

  • No AI detector is 100% accurate; false positives and negatives can occur
  • Results may vary depending on the AI model used to generate text
  • Heavily edited AI-generated text or hybrid human-AI content may be harder to detect
  • Very short texts provide less data for accurate analysis
  • The detector is continuously improving as we gather more data and refine our models
